# System Design

A structured, self-driven study of System Design concepts covering both **Low Level Design (LLD)** and **High Level
Design (HLD)** — from foundational design principles to architecting real-world scalable systems.

This repository contains my implementations, concept notes, and case study solutions written in **Java**, organized
topic by topic as I progress through the curriculum.

---

## What This Covers

The study is structured into two major tracks:

**Low Level Design** focuses on writing clean, maintainable, and extensible code. It covers the SOLID principles, all
major GoF design patterns, UML modelling, and ends with designing real-world systems like Uber, Zomato, and a Parking
Lot from scratch.

**High Level Design** focuses on architecting large-scale distributed systems. It covers scalability, databases,
caching, message queues, microservices, and the tradeoffs involved in designing systems that serve millions of users.
